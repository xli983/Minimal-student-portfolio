Hi, I am Jessica! I'm a rising junior at Georgia Institute of Technology, studying Computational Media with a concentration in Game and Media. My focus areas are game development, technical art, and digital art.

I initially joined Georgia Tech with a major in Physics. Driven by curiosity about the world, I spent my high school years and the first two years of college deeply engaged in studying physics, reading philosophy, and participating in various science competitions. However, there came a day when I realized I could not endure a life without creating art and expressing myself. Thus, decisively, in my sophomore year, I switched my major to Computational Media, reclaiming the art I had set aside in high school for academic pursuits. In this fascinating interdisciplinary field, my scientific mindset and my passion for art have merged together. I aspire to one day enter the film or gaming industry, where I can use my technical skills and creativity to produce meaningful artistic works that I believe in.

  • View Project

    Blender Subway Project

    2023
    Individual Project
    Blender Photoshop 3D Max

    This was a class assignment where we create comics using Blender. Inspired by my interest with subways and thriller movies, I decided to create a comic about a girl trapped in an infinite subway. This is the first page of the comic, with additional pages currently under development. I modeled all the assets for this project and particularly enjoyed modeling the subway, which is a 1:1 model of the oldest version of Line 1 subway in Shanghai. For the first time, I also attempted character modeling using 3D Max, then applied fabric simulation in Blender to create the clothing. Additionally, I developed a geometry node tool for creating hair.

  • View Project

    Rail Transit Line 4

    2023
    Collaborative Duo Project
    Unity3D Blender Photoshop After Effects

    This Unity 3D puzzle game was developed in collaboration with my friend, Will. In the game, you find yourself trapped in an infinite subway, searching for a way out. Throughout this journey, you come to realize that you are in a coma after a suicide attempt and find a reason to live within the confines of the subway. The game is still incomplete, as it was intended to have multiple endings, but currently, only one has been developed.
    The game is inspired by the life of Xu Lizhi, one of my favorite poets. He was known as a migrant worker on the assembly line at Foxconn and a poet who tragically took his own life at the age of 24. This game is created to honor him and all the lower-class laborers.
    One of the most enjoyable parts of this project was creating the illusion of an infinite subway in Unity, achieved through teleportation and shaders to give the impression of being trapped in the subway. I also greatly enjoyed creating the 3D assets for the game, as well as producing cutscenes and the scenery outside the windows using After Effects. Here is a video of playthrough.

  • View Project

    Xing

    2022 - Current
    Startup Project
    JavaScript WebGL Python

    Xing is a sophisticated web-based, cross-platform drawing application that leverages advanced Artificial Intelligence Generated Content (AIGC) algorithms to empower artists in their creative processes.
    As the lead architect behind these AI algorithms, I played a pivotal role in steering Xing's technological direction. Collaborating intimately with multidisciplinary teams, I translated complex business objectives into innovative AI-centric solutions. Beyond my focus on AI, I made significant contributions to the broader software development efforts, crafting essential features such as intuitive drawing tools, precise selection mechanisms, a versatile layer system, dynamic filters, and a comprehensive 3D editor, all through the adept use of JavaScript and WebGL. My involvement was also critical in strategic business planning and the formulation of growth strategies, which were instrumental in securing vital seed funding for the project.
    Currently, Xing is in the alpha testing phase, with ambitious plans to initiate beta testing by May 2024.
